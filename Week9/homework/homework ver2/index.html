Compared to the previous version where a soft rope was used to catch falling balls, this iteration turns the interaction into
a simple throwing game. Instead of catching the balls, the user now needs to put the balls into a basket to gain points.

Step 1: Add a basket and scoring system

In this step, I introduced a basket and a basic score counter to give the interaction a clear goal. When a ball successfully enters
the basket, it is counted as one point and removed from the system. The score is displayed at the top of the screen

Key implementation idea:

  • Build the basket using static physics bodies (left wall, right wall, bottom)
  • Check whether a ball's position is inside the basket area
  • Increase the score when a ball enters

Step 2: Separate ball spawn position and basket location

In the earlier version, the balls fell near the catching area, making it possible to score without much effort. To fix this,
I moved the ball spawn point to the left side of the screen and placed the basket on the right side.

Design change:

  • Balls spawn from the left side
  • The basket is fixed on the right side

Step 3: Replace the soft rope with a rigid plank

In the final step, I replaced the soft, flexible rope with a rigid plank controlled by the thumb and index finger.
The plank does not deform and can be rotated and resized based on finger distance.
This change transforms the interaction from “catching” to “hitting” or “redirecting.” Instead of holding the ball buy the rope,
the user can now actively "hit" the ball toward the basket, creating a more dynamic and game-like experience.

Interaction logic:

  • Thumb and index finger control the plank’s position and angle
  • Finger distance controls the plank’s length
  • The rigid body allows stronger, more decisive interactions with the balls
