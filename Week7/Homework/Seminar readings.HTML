Reading Response

Programming Design Systems – Introduction

Quote 1

“Digital products allow users to interact with their content, and take advantage of motion and animation.”

This line reminds me that digital design is not only about layout or visual style. Unlike printed media, digital products exist over time and respond to user actions.
These changes are part of the design itself. Because of this, a good digital design must include behavior, not just appearance. Motion and interaction help users understand
what is happening and how the system reacts to them.

Quote 2

“Code allows designers to create dynamic systems, not just static outcomes.”

I like this quote because it clearly explains why code is important in digital design. Code helps designers build systems that can change based on input, time, or interaction.
I think by working with code, designers can think in terms of relationships and processes, which is essential for interactive and responsive experiences.

Quote 3

“Throwing a design over the wall for production is a bad legacy of the printed page, and there is no reason for the design process to end with the birth of a product.”

I agree with this idea because I think design should be an ongoing process, not just a final handoff. Prototypes, code, and iteration are essential tools that allow designers
to continue learning from real use, we shouldn't treat production as the end of thinking.

What Do Prototypes Prototype? (Houde & Hill)

Quote 1

“Choosing the right kind of more focused prototype to build is an art in itself…”

This quote matches my experience. When I studied architecture, I used to think that prototype isn't very important, and that the final drawing or model mattered the most.
Prototypes often felt like unfinished or secondary work. After working more with interactive and digital projects, my view has changed. I now understand that a prototype
can becomes powerful when it focuses on one clear question. Also, good prototype helps designers learn faster and make better decisions.

Quote 2

“The goal… is to establish a model that describes any prototype… rather than the prototype’s incidental attributes.”

I read this as a warning against judging prototypes by how polished or finished they look. What matters more is what the prototype is trying to explore or learn, such as role,
look-and-feel, or implementation.

Quote 3

“Prototypes are a means of communication among designers, users, and other stakeholders.”

I like this quote because it shows that prototypes are not only for testing ideas, but also for communication. A prototype helps different people understand the same idea,
even if they have different backgrounds or expectations.
